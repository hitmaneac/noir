# THE GREEN BOOK
### a noir point-and-click — story bible

> *Working title.* Alternates: **The Long Rain**, **Vesper Street**, **A Quiet Kind of Trouble**.

**Logline.** A washed-up private eye takes a missing-person job from a woman in red — and finds the man face-down in the river, a ledger every soul in the city would kill for sewn into his coat, and his own name being typed onto a murder warrant.

**Theme.** What kind of man you are when the easy thing and the right thing are different things. (The hero is named **Mores** on purpose.)

---

## THE HERO — Elias Mores

Private license, one good suit, rent two months past due. A bottle in the bottom drawer he tells himself he's quitting. He was almost something once; now he's the guy you hire when the cops won't look and the lawyers won't help. Decent underneath all the tired — which, in this town, is the most dangerous thing a man can be.

His office is **lvl4**: broken window, green banker's lamp, an ashtray doing the work of three. That room *is* Elias. Players should feel it.

> *"Elias Mores. Even his name sounds like a verdict he hasn't read yet."*

---

## THE CAST — and the lie each one tells

Noir rule: **everybody lies, the client most of all.**

| Character (asset) | Role | Their angle / their lie |
|---|---|---|
| **Vera Lange** — *woman in red* (`woman`) | The client / femme fatale | Says she's hunting for her **brother**, Artie Finch. He is not her brother. She is the schemer at the center of all of it. |
| **Artie Finch** — *the clerk* (`clerk`) | The MacGuffin, made flesh | A mob bookkeeper who copied his boss's accounts into a little green ledger and ran. Found dead in Act I. The whole case is chasing his ghost. |
| **Vince Toranno** — *gangster* (`thug`) | The money & the muscle | Owns the docks, the club, half the badges. Wants the green book burned. "Reasonable" right up until he isn't. |
| **Capt. Russ Brandt** — *corrupt captain* (`cop`) | The law, bought | His name is *in* the ledger. Wants the case closed and Elias gone — and if Elias won't go, he'll do nicely as the man who killed Finch. |
| **Avery Quist** — *crooked lawyer* (`attorney`) | The fixer | Toranno's bagman in a good suit. Offers cash to walk away, then a threat, then a frame. The respectable face of the rot. |
| **Mickey Sloan** — *bartender* (`bartender`) | The chorus / the broker | Neutral ground. Knows everything; says it for a drink and a favor owed. Elias's one almost-honest contact. |
| **Dot Hayes** — *secretary* (`secretary` / `sidekick`) | The Effie Perine | Cynical, loyal, smarter than Elias. Runs the office, does the records legwork, calls him a sap to his face. The story's conscience — the only person who'd cry at his funeral. |

## THE MACGUFFIN — the green book

A leather ledger, hand-copied by Finch: every dirty dollar of Toranno's with a name beside it — including **Capt. R. Brandt**. Hold it and you own the city; that's exactly why it's a death sentence.

Who wants it, and why:
- **Vera** — leverage, and a way out from under Toranno.
- **Toranno** — to burn it before it burns him.
- **Brandt** — to bury it (and the man who found it).
- **Quist** — to trade it up the ladder.
- **The clean DA / the newspapers** — to blow the lid off the whole rotten town.

---

## THE TRUTH (what's really going on)

Vera Lange was **Finch's lover** *and* **Toranno's moll**, playing both ends. She talked timid little Artie into copying the book and running — so the ledger would surface where *she* could grab it and finally have something over Toranno. The plan went sideways: Toranno's people found out Finch had run, and **Toranno had him killed** before Vera could collect.

Now there's a dead man with a priceless book on him and a scheme with no payoff. So Vera hires the one kind of man who can recover it without her fingerprints on it: a down-on-his-luck PI who'll do the legwork, take the heat, and never know he was the cat's-paw. **Elias is the dupe who "finds" it clean.** Everything she tells him is true except the part that matters.

She isn't the killer. She's worse — she's the reason there's a body at all, and she'll let Elias hang for it without losing a night's sleep, unless he figures her out first.

## STRUCTURE — three acts, mapped to the rooms

### ACT I — The Setup
- **Office, rain (lvl4).** Vera walks in, hires Elias to find Artie. Pays double, doesn't ask his rate. Leaves a **photograph** and a **pawn ticket**.
  - *Dot, after she's gone:* "She tipped you double and didn't ask the rate. People only do that when they already know how the story ends."
- **Vesper Street, rainy exterior (lvl1 → redressed).** Finch's tenement. The super, a ransacked room. *Puzzle:* pawn ticket → a locker; photograph → a face Mickey will recognize.
- **The docks, night (lvl2 → redressed, or new exterior).** Elias finds Artie. Dead. The **green book** sewn into the coat lining — and his cover is blown the second he pockets it. *End of Act I.*

### ACT II — Everybody Wants It
- **Mickey's bar (NEW interior).** Mickey reads the photo, points three directions for three favors. Establishes the board. The best dialogue scene in the game.
- **Quist's law office (lvl3 → redressed).** A fat envelope to forget Finch. **Choice beat #1:** take it or refuse. The temperature drops either way.
- **The precinct (NEW interior).** Brandt "invites" Elias in. Warns him off. Elias clocks Finch's case file already typed — *his own name* penciled in the margin. *Object: a glimpse of Brandt's file.*
- **Toranno's club, back room (NEW interior).** The polite version of a beating. Toranno knows Elias has the book. So does everyone now.

### ACT III — The Squeeze & the Choice
- The frame snaps shut: Brandt pins Finch on Elias. **Dot** springs him / hides the book / takes the risk that puts *her* in the line of fire — which is the only thing that's ever made Elias move fast.
- **The reveal:** Vera was never looking for a brother. Elias pieces together the lover-and-moll double play. She made him.
- **The reckoning.** Everyone converges — the office, or the docks in the rain. Elias decides who the book destroys.

## THE ENDINGS (branching — ship both)

The mystery is *who killed Finch* and *where's the book*. The real question is the noir one: **what kind of man is Elias Mores when nobody's watching.**

Vera makes the offer: the book, half the leverage, and her. All he has to do is forget whose lies were whose.

**→ THE HARD ENDING (classic).** Elias turns her in.
> "I won't play the sap for you, angel."

He hands the book to the one clean DA. Brandt and Toranno go down; the headlines scream for a week; the city stays exactly as dirty as it was. He keeps his license and his spine and loses the girl and a piece of himself. Last shot: **Dot lights his cigarette, says nothing, means everything.** Rain.

**→ THE BLEAK ENDING (let her walk).** Elias lets Vera go; the book "disappears."
Nobody hangs, nobody's clean, and he tells himself it was mercy. **Dot doesn't look at him the same.** Bleaker — and arguably more honest.

### Branch logic (engine-ready)
Track two things in state:
1. `hasBook` — does the player still hold the green book at the finale?
2. `choice.vera` — `"turn_in"` or `"let_go"` (the final dialog choice).

| Condition | Ending |
|---|---|
| Book handed to the DA **and** `vera = turn_in` | **Hard** |
| Vera walks (`vera = let_go`) **and/or** the book vanishes | **Bleak** |

Smaller flags that color the road there (flavor, Dot's regard, optional scenes — not separate endings):
- `tookBribe` (Quist, Act II) — easier money now, Mickey trusts you less, an extra squeeze later.
- `protectedDot` vs `usedDot` (Act III) — changes Dot's final beat in either ending.

## HOW IT MAPS TO THE ENGINE

- **Dialog + staging** → interrogations and office confrontations. Use `{ who, text, hero:{x,y,facing}, npc:{x,y,facing} }` so Vera drifts to the window while Elias crosses to the desk.
- **Inventory** → photograph, pawn ticket, the **green book**, Finch's key, the matchbook from the club, the glimpse of Brandt's file. Classic gating: item from scene A unlocks scene B.
- **Action zones** → examine the ashtray, the broken window, the ledger, the file. (lvl4 already has `letter` / `ashtray` / `trash` / `window` zones waiting for lines.)
- **NPCs + green/blue zones** → each character lives in a room; the scene graph is already the exit zones.
- **Choice flags** → store on a small `window.STORY` object the engine reads at the finale.
